## How to read the proposal

Open [`proposal.md`](./proposal.md) directly in GitHub — it renders with full Markdown formatting, including the policy options matrix and implementation table.

The proposal follows the structure required by the assignment brief:

1. **Executive Summary** (~150 words)
2. **Problem Definition and Context** — the four causal channels (task displacement, augmentation, reinstatement, skill-biased technical change) through which AI reshapes work, grounded in the Acemoglu–Restrepo task-based framework
3. **Analysis of Impacts** — employment, wages and polarisation, industry composition, and a distributional/equity lens
4. **Policy Options** — five options evaluated against equity, efficiency, fiscal cost and feasibility, with explicit comparative trade-off discussion
5. **Recommended Package and Implementation Plan** — three-policy package, four-year sequenced rollout, lead agency responsibilities, costs and KPIs
6. **Risks and Mitigations**
7. **Conclusion**
8. **References** (RMIT Harvard style)
9. **AI Use Acknowledgement** (per RMIT academic integrity guidelines)

## Theoretical framework

The proposal applies the **task-based labour-economics framework** of Acemoglu and Restrepo (2019), extended with Autor, Levy and Murnane's (2003) routinisation hypothesis, to analyse how AI reshapes Australian labour markets through four distinct causal channels.

## Evidence base

The proposal draws on Australian and international evidence published in 2003–2025, including:

- Jobs and Skills Australia (2025), *Our Gen AI Transition* and *Jobs and Skills Report 2025*
- Productivity Commission (2025), *Harnessing Data and Digital Technology — Final Report*
- Australian Government (2025), *National AI Plan*
- Acemoglu (2024), *The Simple Macroeconomics of AI*
- Acemoglu & Restrepo (2019), *Automation and New Tasks*
- Brynjolfsson, Li & Raymond (2023), *Generative AI at Work*
- Autor, Levy & Murnane (2003), *The Skill Content of Recent Technological Change*
- PwC Australia (2025), *AI Jobs Barometer*

Full citations in RMIT Harvard style appear in the References section of the proposal.
